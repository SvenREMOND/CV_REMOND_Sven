The different races

The characteristics essentially of morphology and colour retained between generations of cats are usually used to define breeds, whose purity is based on consistency and concordance. with standards. It should be remembered that this notion of race has first of all a descriptive purpose of categorization. arbitrary, rather than a strong biological consistency (only the species shows a homogeneity of which, for certain criteria, anatomical, genetic... the variance is sometimes less than in the population. of a race). The races remain interferential. Inbreeding frequently produces defects. For example, the Deafness is common in cross-bred kittens of white cats. In France, a pedigree cat is a cat with a pedigree. The registration registers of specimens are maintained by different associations such as the American TICA, the ACFA and the CFA, the French LOOF, two international federations, FIFé and WCF or the British GCCF. These associations allow the registration of specimens on strict criteria of genetic origin. Thus any animal whose parents are not listed is excluded. These registrations are paid for. Pedigree cats are a minority and represent according to AFIRAC only 5% of the total cat population. All other domestic cats, those without a pedigree, are considered as alley cats, also called house cats. The number of recognized breeds varies from single to double according to these organizations. Some are very old, like the Siamese or the Turkish Angora, others have been created more recently, like the ragdoll or the peterbald. Humans have also hybridized domestic cats with small felines, giving rise to breeds of such as than Bengal.
